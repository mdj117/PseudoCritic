The Memory of Things

"Poetically portraying the flight of thoughts and mingling of memories" - Tanuska

As a story about the 9/11 attack, The Memory of Things is a wonderful read. The author, Gae Polisner uses her amazing character building skills to create a vivid story with a fascinating adventure. The plot is about Kyle, a boy, who finds an amnesiac on the bridge when he's trying to escape the collapsing Twin Towers. She's on the railing, ready to suicide, ash coating her body. Kyle stops her, takes her home and tries to fix her broken memory. Meanwhile, the reader has insight into the girl's perspective through poetry. This is awesome. The girl, (spoiler alert) who is a ballerina, talks in poetic language, practically in song. Poetry (like her amnesiac thoughts) is broken and doesn't make her thoughts weird and boring, rather, they seem mysterious and thought-provoking. Since the girl doesn’t know her name (till like half the book) Polisner manages to keep the story moving along with descriptions like “Swimmer girl” instead, which is to say, pretty impressive.
